📋 Abstract

We present Quantum Mind, a novel mental health triage system that leverages a Variational Quantum Classifier (VQC) to detect depression risk and mental health crises in students. By mapping 14 psychological and behavioral features onto a 14-qubit quantum circuit, we exploit quantum entanglement to model complex, non-linear symptom interactions that classical methods struggle to capture.

The system integrates neurocognitive games, real-time voice biometric analysis, and AI-powered transcript processing to create a holistic, multimodal assessment pipeline deployed on Google Cloud Run.

⚠️ Problem Statement

The Challenge

Depression is a leading cause of disability among students worldwide. Traditional screening relies on self-report surveys alone, which miss critical behavioral and cognitive signals. Classical ML models struggle with the non-linear entanglement between psychological symptoms — where anxiety, sleep disruption, isolation, and cognitive decline interact in complex feedback loops.

Our Hypothesis

Quantum machine learning can detect complex, non-linear relationships between psychological symptoms (modeled as quantum entanglement) more effectively than classical approaches, enabling earlier and more accurate risk identification.

Key Limitations Addressed

  • Self-report surveys alone miss behavioral signals
  • Classical models underfit symptom interactions
  • No multimodal student screening tools exist
  • Early intervention requires real-time triage

Feature Engineering

Synthetic features derived from cross-feature interactions:

  • Connectivity = Academic − Anxiety − Sleep
  • Memory = Diet − Anxiety − Sleep
  • Bullying = Isolation + Anxiety + Sleep
  • Safety = inv(Substance + Family + Anxiety)
  • Exercise = Sleep − Anxiety + Diet

Quantum Circuit Training

VQC optimization over 100+ steps:

  • Phase 1: Angle embedding via Ry gates
  • Phase 2: 3 StronglyEntanglingLayers
  • Phase 3: Pauli-Z measurement on q₀
  • Output: P(risk) = (⟨Z⟩ + 1) / 2
