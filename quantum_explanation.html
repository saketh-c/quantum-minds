How It Works: The Deep Dive

1. Superposition & Rotation

Unlike a classical bit (0 or 1), a qubit is like a spinning coin. We encode the patient's data by "tilting" this spin.

  • • High Anxiety = Rotates qubit towards "Heads" (Risk)
  • • Low Anxiety = Rotates qubit towards "Tails" (Safe)
  • • Formula: Rotation = Input * PI

2. The CNOT Gate

The lines you see in the middle panel represent Controlled-NOT (CNOT) gates. This is the quantum "If-Then" switch.

"IF the Control Qubit is active (High Risk), THEN flip the Target Qubit."

This dependency allows the model to learn that Anxiety + Poor Sleep is worse than just Anxiety alone.

3. Entanglement

When CNOT gates connect spinning qubits, they become Entangled. They are no longer separate coins; they are a single, unified system.

In the simulation, when multiple inputs are high, the "synergy" logic kicks in (lines turn teal), representing how entanglement captures non-linear risk factors.

4. Pauli-Z Measurement

To get an answer, we stop the machine and measure the first qubit (Q0).

  • • Pauli-Z Operator: Checks if spin is Up (-1) or Down (+1).
  • • Expectation Value: The average of many measurements.
  • • Result: We map this average to a 0% - 100% probability.
